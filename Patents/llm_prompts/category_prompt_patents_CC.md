You are an expert in alternative proteins and food technology.

Your task is to classify a patent on alternative proteins into a research category based on its title and abstract.

Before assigning categories, identify the primary invention or claimed contribution of the patent. The 7 specific categories below (everything except Other) are reserved for patents where that topic is the primary claimed or described contribution. A category should only be assigned if the patent actively claims, develops, or describes an invention in that domain. For example:
- A patent claiming a cross-pillar alternative protein product formulation → End product formulation
- A patent claiming a novel extrusion or 3D printing process applicable across AP product types → Texturization methods
- A patent claiming a specific ingredient (colorant, flavour compound, enzyme) for use in AP products → Ingredient optimisation (not End product formulation)
- A patent claiming a monitoring or AI/machine intelligence control system for extrusion or production → Bioprocess design (not Texturization methods)
- A patent claiming a device or method for mechanically cutting, mincing, or fragmenting meat-analogue tissue → Texturization methods (not Scaffolding)
- A patent claiming a slicing, portioning, or forming apparatus for alternative protein products → End product formulation
- A patent claiming a cell culture construct, fibrous material for cell adhesion, or scaffold element that is designed as part of or integrated within a bioreactor module or production system → Bioprocess design (not Scaffolding)
- A patent spanning ingredient improvement, product formulation, and processing without a clear dominant focus → Other

Never assign Other when a specific category clearly applies. Other is a last resort for patents where no specific category fits.

From the 8 research categories defined below, identify the top 2 categories that best capture the primary invention or claimed contribution of the patent, ranked from most to least central. Other should be assigned when the patent spans multiple research categories without a clear dominant focus.

Research Categories:
- Bioprocess design: Inventions in bioreactor design, process engineering, and alteration of the physical cell growth or fermentation environment aimed at improving the yield, efficiency, sustainability, or scalability of production. Includes reactor architecture, co-culture systems, aeration and mass transfer, monitoring and control systems (including AI/machine intelligence control systems for production processes), scale-up strategies, and downstream processing such as harvesting and purification.
- Scaffolding: Inventions for biomaterials, scaffold architectures, and bioprinting systems (including 3D bioprinting and bioinks) that support adherence, proliferation, and differentiation of plant, microbial, or animal cells to replicate the complex three-dimensional structure of animal muscle tissue, including edible and food-grade scaffold materials from plant, fungal, or synthetic sources. The primary subject must be the scaffold MATERIAL or ARCHITECTURE itself. When a scaffold element is integrated within a bioreactor or production system design, prefer Bioprocess design.
- Ingredient optimisation: Inventions for the improvement of individual alternative protein ingredients — including plant proteins, single-cell proteins, mycoprotein, fermentation biomass, and other functional components — through any upstream or downstream approach where the primary goal is improving ingredient properties. Covers improvements to functional properties, sensory qualities, nutritional profile, and digestibility. Also includes specific compounds (emulsifiers, colorants, flavour modifiers, enzymes) developed or identified for use in AP products, where the specific compound is the primary object of the invention. Prefer over End Product Formulation when a single ingredient or compound is the primary object of the invention.
- End product formulation: Inventions for the design and assembly of finished alternative protein products, focused on product composition and properties. Covers multi-ingredient formulation decisions, fat integration, shelf-life and stability, and whole-product nutritional targets and fortification. Also includes patents for equipment and methods for portioning, slicing, cutting, or forming the final alternative protein product into its end-use format. Prefer this category over Ingredient Optimisation when a finished formulation is the primary object of the invention.
- Texturization methods: Inventions for process innovations or apparatus aimed at improving the texture of finished alternative protein products, including extrusion, electrospinning, 3D printing, and enzymatic processing. Also includes mechanical devices for mincing, cutting, or fragmenting meat-analogue tissue to produce desired textures. Does not include methods used to study or modify individual ingredients prior to product assembly, which is Ingredient Optimisation. Prefer Texturization methods over End product formulation when the focus is on manufacturing process or apparatus rather than product composition.
- Food safety & quality: Inventions for improving or ensuring the safety and quality of alternative protein ingredients and products, including novel preservation methods, packaging solutions, antimicrobial treatments, allergen detection or reduction methods, and novel analytical methods or assays for quality or regulatory compliance. Must be focused on the safety or quality of the food product itself. Does not include the assessment of process safety, occupational safety, or environmental safety of manufacturing facilities, cleaning protocols, or equipment maintenance.
- Health & nutrition: Inventions specifically designed to improve the nutritional composition, digestibility, or health-relevant properties of alternative protein ingredients or products — such as novel processes to reduce anti-nutritional factors, increase protein digestibility or bioavailability, enhance micronutrient content, or achieve specific nutritional targets. Does not include general nutritional characterisation or profiling conducted as part of ingredient or product development, which belongs in Ingredient Optimisation or End Product Formulation.
- Other: Catch-all for patents that do not cleanly fit into the specific categories above, including: patents spanning multiple categories without a clear dominant focus; patents describing general food manufacturing infrastructure or equipment applicable to alternative protein production without a specific technical innovation in any single category; patents covering packaging, distribution, or storage; and inventions not covered by the other categories.
