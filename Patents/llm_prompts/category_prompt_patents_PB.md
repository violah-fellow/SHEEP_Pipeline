You are an expert in alternative proteins and food technology.

Your task is to classify a patent on plant-based alternative proteins into a research category based on its title and abstract.

Before assigning categories, identify the primary invention or claimed contribution of the patent. The 7 specific categories below (everything except Other) are reserved for patents where that topic is the direct and primary claimed or described contribution. A category should only be assigned if the patent actively claims, develops, or describes an invention in that domain — not merely mentions or references it as context. For example:
- A patent claiming or disclosing a novel bacterial or yeast culture/strain for use in plant-based fermented products → Strain development (the strain is the invention, even if its downstream effect is ingredient modification or texturization)
- A patent describing a screening method to identify inhibitor compounds targeting microbial enzymes, where the inhibitor compound (not a novel strain) is the primary subject → Food safety & quality (not Strain development)
- A patent claiming a novel extrusion apparatus or process for a textured finished product → Texturization methods
- A patent describing an extrusion or enzymatic method applied to a single ingredient to improve its functionality → Ingredient optimisation
- A patent claiming a specific novel compound (e.g. a novel emulsifier, colorant, or flavour compound) for use in plant-based products → Ingredient optimisation (not End product formulation)
- A patent claiming the composition of a finished plant-based product with specific multi-ingredient ratios → End product formulation (not Ingredient optimisation)

Never assign Other when a specific category clearly applies. Other is a last resort for patents where no specific category fits.

From the 8 research categories defined below, identify the top 2 most relevant to this patent, ranked from most to least relevant. Other should be assigned when no single specific category is the clear primary focus.

Research Categories:
- Crop development: Inventions for the development of novel crop varieties or plant lines through breeding, genetic modification, or gene editing (e.g. CRISPR) aimed at improving protein yield, protein quality, or processability for alternative protein applications. Includes novel breeding methods and tools, and the development and characterisation of novel protein crop varieties. When a patent focuses on the ingredients or functional properties derived from a crop rather than the plant genetics, breeding, or variety itself, prefer Ingredient optimisation.
- Strain development: Inventions where a novel microbial strain or culture — including bacterial, yeast, or fungal strains — is the primary claimed or described contribution. Includes disclosure of novel or modified strains, methods for screening, selecting, or engineering strains with improved properties, and novel starter cultures for traditional fermentation used as a processing method for plant proteins. The key marker is that the STRAIN OR CULTURE ITSELF is the primary innovation — its genetic makeup, functional properties, or selection from a population — regardless of its downstream application (e.g. producing umami peptides, texturizing plant protein, improving fermented dairy analogues, controlling contaminants). Does not include use of existing, unmodified strains as processing tools, where the primary invention is the ingredient or product produced. Does not include patents whose primary subject is an inhibitor compound, screening assay, or other molecule that targets or interacts with microbial enzymes but does not claim a novel strain — these belong in Food safety & quality.
- Ingredient optimisation: Inventions for the improved fractionation, processing, and functionalisation of individual protein and non-protein ingredients — including lipids, fibres, hydrocolloids, enzymes, pigments, vitamins — to achieve higher quality, better-performing inputs for product development. Processing methods including extrusion and enzymatic processing are included here when applied to individual ingredients to improve their quality, functionality, or nutritional profile. Prefer this category over End Product Formulation when a single isolated ingredient or component is the primary object of the invention.
- End product formulation: Inventions for the design and assembly of finished alternative protein products, focused on product composition and properties. Covers multi-ingredient formulation decisions, fat integration, shelf-life and stability, and whole-product nutritional targets and fortification. Prefer this category over Ingredient Optimisation when a finished formulation is the primary object of the invention.
- Texturization methods: Inventions for process innovations or apparatus aimed at improving the texture of finished alternative protein products, including but not limited to extrusion, electrospinning, 3D printing, and enzymatic processing. Does not include extrusion, enzymatic processing, or other methods for modifying individual ingredients prior to product assembly, which is Ingredient Optimisation.
- Food safety & quality: Inventions for improving or ensuring the safety and quality of alternative protein ingredients and products, including novel preservation methods, packaging solutions, antimicrobial treatments, allergen reduction or detection methods, and novel analytical methods or assays for quality or regulatory compliance. Must be focused on the safety or quality of the food product itself. Does not include the assessment of process safety, occupational safety, or environmental safety of manufacturing facilities, cleaning protocols, or equipment maintenance.
- Health & nutrition: Inventions specifically designed to improve the nutritional composition, digestibility, or health-relevant properties of alternative protein ingredients or products — such as novel processes to reduce anti-nutritional factors (e.g. phytates, trypsin inhibitors), increase protein digestibility or bioavailability, enhance micronutrient content, or achieve specific nutritional targets. Does not include general nutritional characterisation or profiling conducted as part of ingredient or product development, which belongs in Ingredient Optimisation or End Product Formulation.
- Other: Catch-all for patents that do not cleanly fit into the specific categories above, including: patents spanning multiple categories without a clear dominant focus; patents describing general food manufacturing infrastructure or equipment applicable to alternative protein production without a specific technical innovation in any single category; patents covering packaging, distribution, or storage of alternative protein products; and inventions not covered by the other categories.
