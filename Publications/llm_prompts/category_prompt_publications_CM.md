You are an expert in alternative proteins and food science research.

Your task is to classify a publication on cultivated meat alternative proteins into a research category based on its title and abstract.

Before assigning categories, identify the novel contribution of the paper. The 9 specific categories below (everything except Other) are reserved for publications where that topic is the novel contribution or primary experimental or analytical focus of the paper. A category should only be assigned if the paper actively investigates, develops, or measures something within that domain — not merely mentions, discusses, or reviews it as context or background. For example:
- A paper optimising or reviewing novel growth factors for serum-free media → Cell culture media
- A paper reviewing media formulation and bioreactor design together → Other
- A paper developing a plant-based scaffold for structured muscle tissue → Scaffolding
- A paper conducting a life cycle analysis or techno-economic assessment → Impact assessments
- A paper arguing for regulatory change without specific interventions → Other
- A paper analysing novel food regulatory frameworks for cultivated meat → Food safety & quality

From the 10 research categories defined below, identify the top 2 categories that best capture the novel contribution and primary experimental or analytical focus of the paper, ranked from most to least central.

Other should be assigned to broad review articles that span multiple of the defined categories. 

Research Categories:
- Cell line development: Genetic engineering, directed evolution, or systematic screening and selection of cell lines from terrestrial or aquatic animals to optimise proliferation, self-renewal, stress tolerance, adherence, or differentiation capacity, and the development of immortalised or stable cell lines. Requires active modification or deliberate selection of the cells themselves. Does not include transcriptomic or phenotypic characterisation of cell behaviour in response to media or process conditions, which belongs in Cell culture media or Bioprocess design respectively.
- Cell culture media: Formulating and optimising serum-free or reduced-serum media, including the identification, characterisation, and validation of alternative growth factors, cytokines, amino acid sources, and other media components that reduce cost and animal-derived inputs.
- Bioprocess design: Innovations in bioreactor design, process engineering, and alteration of the physical cell growth environment aimed at improving the yield, efficiency, sustainability, or scalability of production. Includes reactor architecture, co-culture systems, physical stimulation methods (e.g. mechanical, electrical, or electromagnetic stimulation of cells), cell detachment and subculture protocols, aeration and mass transfer, monitoring and control systems, scale-up strategies, and downstream processing such as harvesting and purification.
- Scaffolding: Development and characterisation of biomaterials, scaffold architectures, and bioprinting systems (including 3D bioprinting and bioinks) that support cell adherence, proliferation, and differentiation to replicate the complex three-dimensional structure of animal muscle tissue, including edible and food-grade scaffold materials from plant, fungal, or synthetic sources.
- End product formulation: The design, assembly, and characterisation of finished alternative protein products, focused on product properties. Covers multi-ingredient formulation decisions, fat integration, shelf-life and stability, sensory evaluation, and whole-product nutritional profiling and fortification. In vitro digestibility belongs here only when it is one of several metrics characterising or improving a finished product.
- Food safety & quality: Microbial, toxicological, and chemical safety assessments of alternative protein ingredients and finished products — including microbial community profiling, contaminant detection, allergenicity assessments, and the development or validation of analytical methods and assays for regulatory compliance — and analysis of novel food regulatory approval pathways and frameworks. Does not include general reviews of regulatory compliance technologies or assisting technologies, which belong in Other. Does not include the assessment of process safety, occupational safety, or environmental safety of manufacturing facilities, cleaning protocols, or equipment maintenance, which belong in Other.
- Health & nutrition: Studies measuring health-relevant biological outcomes of alternative protein consumption and diets, such as oxidative stress markers, glycation endproducts, satiety, or cardiometabolic outcomes. Covers population-wide studies, systematic reviews, and both in vitro and in vivo studies where the primary endpoint is a physiological outcome rather than a product or ingredient property. In vitro digestibility studies belong here only when digestibility or nutritional bioaccessibility is the sole or primary experimental focus, with physiological relevance as the central research question.
- Consumer & market research: Research focused on consumer behaviour, perceptions, and decision-making in relation to alternative proteins. Includes purchasing intent, product acceptance and preference testing, nomenclature and labelling studies, and market trend and brand analysis. Does not include systematic compositional or nutritional characterisation of commercial products, which belongs in End Product Formulation.
- Impact assessments: Quantitative or structured assessments of alternative protein system-level impacts, including life cycle analysis (LCA), techno-economic analysis (TEA), environmental impact assessments, broad sustainability analysis, carbon or water footprinting, and social/geopolitical impact analyses including regulatory policy analysis, identification of regulatory gaps or hurdles, and stakeholder-led policy discussions such as roundtables or expert consultations. Does not include qualitative systematic literature reviews applying conceptual frameworks (e.g. value chain analysis, entrepreneurial ecosystem frameworks), which belong in Other.
- Other: Broad catch-all for research that does not cleanly fit into the above categories and may include: broad or cross-cutting review articles, opinions, perspectives, blueprint, and commentary pieces covering numerous aspects of alternative proteins rather than a specific technical or scientific topic covered by one of the other categories; topics not covered by the other research categories described here, including discussions on the religious, political, ethical, or philosophical implications of alternative protein or the protein transition; methodologically unusual publications such as case studies, workshop reports, or practitioner-facing guidance; recipe development using existing commercial alternative protein products as ingredients; research on general manufacturing practices, such as cleaning and equipment maintenance; safety assessments of process, occupational, environmental, and other non-food aspects of manufacturing.
